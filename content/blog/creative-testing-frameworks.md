## Why unstructured testing plateaus fast

Running a handful of ad variations inside one ad set feels like testing, but it rarely isolates what's actually driving performance. Hook, angle, and offer all move at once, so wins are hard to repeat and losses are hard to diagnose.

## Separate the variables that matter

- **Hook** — the first three seconds of a video, or the headline of a static. Test this in isolation before touching anything else.
- **Angle** — the underlying argument for why the product matters (price, status, convenience, urgency).
- **Offer** — the specific deal or framing (percent off, bundle, free shipping threshold).

## A testing cadence that scales

Structure tests in weekly waves: one new hook variant per top-performing angle, refreshed on a fixed schedule rather than reactively. This keeps creative fatigue from quietly eating into ROAS between structured refreshes.

## What this looks like white-label

When this is run as a resold service, the agency's client sees a clean weekly creative report — win/loss by hook, not a raw ad-level export — with the underlying testing framework staying invisible to the end client.

## Start with scope, not price

Most white-label partnerships break down not because of price, but because scope was never pinned down. Before signing anything, agencies should get clear answers on exactly what's included per channel, per account, and what counts as a change request.

## Access is a trust problem, not just a technical one

Ask how the partner handles ad account and analytics access: is it scoped to only what's needed, is there an NDA and non-compete in place before anything is touched, and what happens to access the day the partnership ends.

## Reporting has to carry your brand

Confirm — in writing — that dashboards, decks and invoices your client sees will carry your agency's name, not the partner's. This is the detail that most often gets assumed rather than confirmed.

## The questions worth asking directly

1. What's the fixed monthly scope per channel, and what triggers a change order?
2. Who is my single point of contact, and what's the expected response time?
3. Is there a written non-compete and no-poaching clause before any account access is granted?
4. What does client-facing reporting look like, and whose branding is on it?
5. What's the offboarding process if the partnership ends?
